Food Security: Farming and Food Packs

The foundation in partnership with KwaMafu Cooperative is promoting crop and vegetable farming at Entsimekweni Village. The project started in 2020 with planting 15 hectares of land. The following were grown, maize, watermelon, pumpkin, potatoes and beans. The plan is to grow more crops and vegetables over bigger area of land. The foundation is also partnering with Youth Health Africa to expand vegetable farming initiatives by young people. In 2020 in the middle of the COVID-19 pandemic, the foundation distributed food packs to the needy members of the community. Youth Health Africa in partnership with FeedSA further distributed 250 food packs in the village. These are welcomed measures that temporarily help community members who are trapped in poverty.


Ntsimeko Resource Centre

The foundation runs a resource centre at Entsimekweni Village in partnership with Youth Health Africa since March 2021. There are two young people who are doing their YES4Youth sponsored youth work experience who run the resource centre. The resource centre provides internet, printing, copying and library services to the community members. The resource centre helps young people searching for employment opportunities. It also helps pupils who need access to internet to do homework projects. The resource centre operates from one room and the need is to expand the resource centre to a bigger space. There are about 800 people who have been served by the resource centre since it was established in March 2021.


Future Projects


Water

The villages struggle to get regular supplies of water . However, even those with access to piped water supply, this can be very irregular with long periods of time without any water supply. This is of course very challenging given COVID-19 pandemic and the need to wash hands in order to prevent spread of COVID-19. The area is also known to have long periods of drought. The plan is to assist villages with borehole drilling, water tanks and purification systems so that they can access water when taps run dry. Water is even more important for establishment of vegetable gardens.


Education: Numeracy and Literacy

Eastern Cape continues to perform poorly as measured by matric results. The foundation aims to initiate community based projects to support learners with after school activities focusing on numeracy and literacy. The best performers who come from struggling households will be assisted with bursaries and scholarships.


Sports and Recreation

Sports facilities are very rare in the villages except for soccer fields which are also poorly maintained. The foundation aims to encourage sports and recreation in the villages. It aims to mobilise resources to build sports facilities in order to encourage participation and competition.


Environment: Recycling

Recycling provides opportunities for some but also encourages clean environment. The foundation seeks to encourage villagers to keep their village clean while benefiting from recycling.


Other Projects

RThe foundation will support and fund projects and businesses started by individuals and groups in the villages with the aim of addressing unemployment and poverty. These might include but not limited to sewing, baking, selling of products and services, etc. Most of the villagers have great ideas that can help them but they lack resources and funding to start something.

Our Partners

KF aims to collaborate and partner with various stakeholders and community based structure to implement its projects. The implementation of poverty alleviation initiatives requires a combination of approaches and expertise. Some partners will bring experience and expertise in implementation of such interventions. KF will mobilise funding and other resources with individuals, corporates, donors and other organisations in order to bring about change and development. Traditional leadership under the Chiefs and Headmen are important in the rural areas. KF has established relationships with traditional leadership from its current projects. KF will also seek to partner and collaborate with government. Government remains vital to socio-economic development.

Where We Work

Joe Gqabi District Municipality in the Eastern Cape Province is the 10th poorest district in South Africa. Eastern Cape Province is the second poorest province in South Africa after Limpopo Province. Unemployment and poverty are very high with most of the villagers depending on social grants provided by government.

Entsimekweni Village which is the main area of activities for the foundation has about 16,000 people. The village is under Chief Mehlomakulu. The village is surrounded by beautiful mountains and rivers. It is home to famous Eskepeni Waterfall. Eskepeni Waterfall has potential to be a tourist attraction area.
